Trojan malware, also known as Trojan Horse, is a type of malicious software that
disguises itself as a legitimate program in order to trick users into installing it on
their computer systems. Once the Trojan is installed, it can execute a variety of harmful
actions, such as stealing personal information, downloading additional malware, or
providing unauthorized access to the infected computer.

One of the major challenges of Trojan malware is its ability to evade traditional
security defenses. For example, it can be spread through phishing scams, infected email
attachments, and malicious websites. To avoid falling victim to a Trojan attack, it is
important to be cautious when downloading and installing new software, being wary of
suspicious emails and links, and keeping your computer’s operating system and
installed programs up-to-date.

In the event that a Trojan is discovered on a computer system, it is important to
take immediate action to remove it. This can typically be achieved by running a
full scan with a reputable antivirus program and deleting any detected malware.
However, in some cases, the infected files may be deeply embedded and difficult to
remove. In such situations, it may be necessary to completely reformat the hard
drive and reinstall the operating system to fully eradicate the malware. It is
also important to change any compromised passwords and monitor financial accounts
for any suspicious activity.
